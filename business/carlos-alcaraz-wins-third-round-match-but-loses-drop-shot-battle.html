Carlos Alcaraz wins third-round match but loses drop-shot battle

Carlos Alcaraz advances to the Australian Open fourth round, defeating Corentin Moutet in straight sets. Despite the win, he admits to losing the drop-shot battle.

Carlos Alcaraz defeated Corentin Moutet in straight sets to advance to the fourth round of the 2026 Australian Open in an exciting third-round match. The world No. 1 acknowledged losing the drop-shot fight, a crucial part of the match that excited the crowd, despite the victory. Alcaraz's tenacity was demonstrated during the match, which is renowned for its thrilling rallies and tactical exchanges, but Moutet's deft play kept the Spanish star on his toes the entire time.

The Power of Alcaraz’s Play: Dominance on the Court

Alcaraz displayed his typical strength, speed, and athleticism throughout the game. The Spaniard's relentless attacks and aggressive play at the baseline were too much for Moutet to handle. Another key component of his strategy was the 2022 US Open champion's serve. It let Moutet score points more rapidly and made his serve more difficult. Alcaraz's ability to dominate rallies and capitalize on his opening serves allowed him to win by a significant margin. Because of his powerful forehands and accurate backhands, Moutet spent the most of the game playing defense. Moutet was skilled in many aspects of the game, but one way he demonstrated his intelligence was through his drop shots.

The Drop-Shot Battle: Moutet’s Clever Strategy

One of the event's most distinctive and captivating features was the drop-shot competition between Moutet and Alcaraz. Alcaraz was forced to cover the entire court by Moutet's deft use of drop shots, which are renowned for their great range. Alcaraz had powerful groundstrokes that were nothing like these gentle, angled shots. The Spaniard had to shift his position fast and sprint in the direction of the net. Moutet's ability to conceal and position his drop shots made it difficult for Alcaraz to completely counter them, despite Alcaraz's reputation for speed and agility. Despite his best efforts, Alcaraz acknowledged that he lost some of those drop-shot duels because he was unable to predict the Frenchman's next move. Rather than merely hitting powerful shots from the baseline, Moutet's ability to alter the depth and speed of his drop shots added interest to the action.

Alcaraz’s Response: A Learning Experience

Although he acknowledged that Moutet was superior to him at drop shots, Alcaraz prevailed in straight sets. For a player who often dominates most of his games, particularly in fast-paced baseline rallies, the drop-shot task was an unexpected test. However, Alcaraz's mental toughness and concentration allowed him to maintain his advantage and win the game without allowing his drop-shot issues to detract from his overall performance.

Alcaraz learned a lot from this match, especially since he wants to improve his game as the tournament gets harder. Alcaraz will probably pay attention to how to deal with these tricky shot variations so that he is ready for future opponents who might use similar tactics.

Outlook..

Carlos Alcaraz's win at the Australian Open in 2026 shows how good he is, and the loss in the drop-shot exchange shows how tough the competition was. Tommy Paul will take on Alcaraz in the fourth round. People anticipate that the young Spaniard will put in much more effort to improve as he approaches winning a Grand Slam. Fans are eager to watch Alcaraz's performance in one of the most difficult and prestigious tennis competitions as the Australian Open progresses.
