Source Methodology

PR Primespot strives to show readers how we collect, verify, and attribute information, ensuring transparency and accuracy throughout our reporting process.

Last Updated: June 5, 2026

How Reporting Begins

Our journalists start with verifiable material, prioritizing official documents, public records, statements from credible sources, and firsthand reporting.

Initial drafts are carefully reviewed to avoid assumptions. If certain facts cannot be fully verified, language is adjusted until we can confirm the information.

Source Hierarchy & Verification

We prioritize primary sources over secondary reporting. Official statements, filings, and public data are preferred. Repetition from other outlets is cross-checked for accuracy.

  • Primary documents and firsthand accounts are prioritized.
  • Secondary reports are used cautiously and not repeated if uncertain.
  • Legal, chronological, or numerical context is verified against authoritative records.

Anonymous Sources & Confidential Background

Anonymous sources are only used when public interest demands it and no alternative is available. We provide context to help readers understand the credibility of the information without exposing the source unnecessarily.

Documents, Media & Data

Documents, audio, video, and data are reviewed carefully to ensure authenticity and relevance. All supporting materials are analyzed to prevent misinterpretation or misleading conclusions.

Source Notes, Attribution & Links

Trust-sensitive reporting includes clear attribution to allow readers to evaluate sources. Links to public documents, statements, or institutional resources are provided when appropriate.

Handling Uncertainty & Updates

  • We avoid converting uncertainty into definitive claims.
  • Analysis is distinguished from assertion.
  • Updates are made when better sourcing or records become available.
  • If a claim remains unresolved, we note it clearly instead of suggesting a settled conclusion.

What This Policy Does Not Mean

This policy does not require disclosing every confidential source or every step of reporting. It ensures readers understand the evidence supporting the story without compromising safety, privacy, or journalistic integrity.
