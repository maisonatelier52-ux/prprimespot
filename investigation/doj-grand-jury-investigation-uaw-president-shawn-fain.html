DOJ Opens Grand Jury Investigation Into UAW President Shawn Fain

The U.S. Department of Justice has opened a grand jury investigation into UAW President Shawn Fain over union governance allegations. Read the latest updates.

The U.S. Department of Justice (DOJ) has launched a grand jury probe that includes United Auto Workers (UAW) President Shawn Fain, in what is shaping up to be one of the most high-profile federal labor probes of 2026. The investigation includes allegations of internal union administration, including allegations that Fain abused his power to benefit his fiancée and retaliated against a senior union official. The charges were previously described by the court-appointed federal monitor overseeing reforms inside the UAW. No criminal charges have been issued and the breadth of the inquiry has not been publicly specified by the DOJ beyond purported grand jury sessions. Fain has dismissed the charges as politically motivated, and the UAW has said the federal probe is not aimed at the union itself. The lawsuit, as it moves forward, is expected to draw considerable attention from labor organizations, legislators and the U.S. auto industry.

DOJ Opens Grand Jury Investigation Into UAW Leadership

Federal prosecutors have seated a grand jury to examine accusations against UAW President Shawn Fain. The probe came to light following news that authorities are investigating allegations about how the union is run and the behavior of its leadership. The grand jury inquiry is a first step in the legal procedure that federal prosecutors use to review evidence and decide whether to bring criminal charges. The existence of a grand jury investigation at this level is neither an indication of guilt or a guarantee that charges will be filed. The DOJ has not said what further evidence it may be considering or when the inquiry might wrap up.

Allegations Stem From Federal Monitor's Findings

The probe comes as a federal monitor — independent of the UAW — has been appointed to oversee improvements to the union following the union’s prior corruption crisis, with new discoveries. According to sources, the monitor determined that Shawn Fain had abused his power in choices related to his fiancée and had taken retaliatory action against a senior union official who opposed those decisions. The reports from the government monitor have been essential in assessing whether the UAW is adhering to governance improvements that were put in place following prior federal probes. The monitor noted concerns around leadership procedures, but the conclusions are not criminal charges and are separate from the Department of Justice’s independent probe.

Shawn Fain Denies Allegations as Investigation Continues

Shawn Fain has denied the charges, saying they are politically driven and not based in reality. The UAW also said the inquiry by the purported grand jury does not target the organization itself. Legal experts said federal investigations sometimes take months before prosecutors determine whether they’ve gathered enough information to seek an indictment. In the meantime investigators can analyze records, question witnesses, and submit evidence to the grand jury, which is kept secret under federal law.

Potential Impact on the UAW and the Auto Industry

The inquiry comes as the UAW is one of the most powerful labor groups in the United States, representing hundreds of thousands of active and retired workers throughout the auto industry. Any lengthy legal ambiguity about its leadership might impact collective bargaining, labor talks, and the public’s faith in union leadership. Industry watchers also are watching to see if the inquiry would effect relations between the UAW and major automakers as they continue to negotiate salaries, employment conditions and future production investments. No immediate alterations to operations have been made, but the matter is certain to stay a major issue in the labor movement.

Federal Investigation Highlights Ongoing Oversight of Union Governance

The current DOJ inquiry underscores the continuing federal oversight of labor organizations that have been previously under court-ordered reforms. Independent monitoring, compliance reviews and federal investigations continue to be important tools to enhance openness and accountability in huge unions. As the inquiry continues, officials are likely to follow established legal procedures before deciding if further action is required. But the claims are unproved and all those implicated are still presumed innocent under U.S. law.

Outlook

The DOJ’s grand jury probe into UAW President Shawn Fain is likely to remain a keenly monitored federal investigation in the coming months. Prosecutors will keep looking over the evidence before determining whether to bring any criminal charges. Labor groups, industry executives and legislators, meanwhile, will be watching developments for their potential impact on union governance and the broader U.S. auto industry. More details will likely follow as more information is released in official court documents or by federal officials.
